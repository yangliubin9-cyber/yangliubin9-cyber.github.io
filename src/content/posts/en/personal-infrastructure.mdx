## Write in your native language first

When you maintain a blog over time, the expensive part is not deployment. It is expression. Writing in your native language is usually faster and preserves more nuance.

That is why I prefer a bilingual workflow where Chinese comes first and English follows.

## AI behaves more like a second editor

I do not think a model should replace authorial judgment, but it is excellent at a few tasks:

- Drafting an English version from the Chinese source.
- Keeping terminology consistent.
- Compressing awkward phrases into cleaner ones.
- Reworking titles, excerpts, and metadata.

That turns bilingual publishing from writing twice into reviewing once more.

## A practical workflow

<aside className="mdx-note">
  Once you add your preferred model API later, the current content structure is already ready for automation.
</aside>

1. Write the source article in `src/content/posts/zh`.
2. Keep the same `translationKey` and `pathSlug`.
3. Use AI to generate an English draft in `src/content/posts/en`.
4. Finish with a human review for tone and accuracy.

## Why this works well with GitHub Pages

It is still a file-based workflow, still completely free, and naturally versioned with Git. The content behaves like code: visible, reviewable, and maintainable.
