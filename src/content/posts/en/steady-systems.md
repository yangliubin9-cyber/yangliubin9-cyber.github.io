## Complexity slowly erodes the desire to publish

Many sites do not fail because the author runs out of ideas. They fail because maintenance gets heavier over time. Every new post starts to feel like deployment work instead of writing.

## Light does not mean weak

The real strength of a static site is that complexity moves to build time instead of request time. The author works with clean files, and the reader gets a fast, stable page.

## Treat your blog like an asset, not a campaign page

Once you see it that way, many decisions become obvious:

- Pick free infrastructure you can maintain for years.
- Keep URLs and language structure stable.
- Use Git and content files as the backbone of your knowledge base.
